I am a CS PhD student at Vanderbilt University, advised by Dr. Jie Ying Wu at [MAPLE Labs](https://vu-maple-lab.github.io), where we develop algorithms and systems to enhance robot-assisted surgeries. My research aims to bridge 3D scene understanding, perception, and robot learning at scale specifically for surgical robots. I focus on two directions: (1) photorealistic and physics-grounded synthetic surgical data generation to train and evaluate 3D vision/scene understanding models, and (2) multimodal learning methods, such as RGB-D pretraining, to help models understand both semantic information and spatial geometry in endoscopic scenes. Ultimately, my long-term goal is to develop robust embodied surgical AI that reduces surgeon workload and expands access to high-quality care, including and especially in low-resource communities and hospitals. 

For collaboration, party invites, or any other inquiries, please contact me at john.j.han@vanderbilt.edu. I would love to get to know you and your work. Soli Deo Gloria!
